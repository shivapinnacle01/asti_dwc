Course Overview

Geology, drilling operations, heat transport, and production engineering are just a few of the important topics covered in this program, which provides a thorough understanding of petroleum engineering. It equips students to use useful tools and advanced technologies to address real-world problems in upstream oil and gas exploration.

Course Objective

  • Provide deep technical knowledge in core petroleum engineering subjects.
  • Equip students with tools for problem-solving in drilling and production.
  • Emphasize sustainable practices in hydrocarbon extraction.
  • Develop hands-on experience using real-world tools, simulations, and case studies.

Program Modules

Title Duration Description
Module 1: Introduction to Petroleum Engineering 4 Hours An introduction to the petroleum industry, focusing on the exploration, extraction, and production of oil and gas, and the role of petroleum engineers.
Module 2: Reservoir Engineering 6 Hours Covers the principles of reservoir behavior, fluid dynamics, and techniques for evaluating and optimizing the production of oil and gas reservoirs.
Module 3: Drilling Engineering 8 Hours Focuses on the design and operation of drilling processes for the exploration and production of oil and gas, including drilling fluids, equipment, and safety.
Module 4: Production Engineering 6 Hours Examines the techniques and equipment used to optimize oil and gas production, including well completion, artificial lift, and production system design.
Module 5: Well Testing and Evaluation 6 Hours Teaches methods for evaluating well performance through testing, including pressure analysis and flow testing, to optimize production.
Module 6: Petroleum Geology and Geophysics 6 Hours Introduces the geological and geophysical principles used in the exploration and development of petroleum resources, including seismic methods and subsurface mapping.
Module 7: Enhanced Oil Recovery Techniques 4 Hours Covers advanced methods for increasing the extraction of oil from reservoirs, such as water flooding, gas injection, and thermal recovery techniques..
Module 8: Health, Safety, and Environmental Management 4 Hours Covers advanced methods for increasing the extraction of oil from reservoirs, such as water flooding, gas injection, and thermal recovery techniques..
Module 9: Petroleum Economics and Project Management 4 Hours Provides an overview of the economic principles and project management techniques used in petroleum engineering to evaluate the financial viability and risks of oil and gas projects.

It is the field that deals with exploring, extracting, and producing oil and natural gas, using engineering principles and technologies.

Who can apply for this course?

Anyone with a background or interest in engineering, geology, energy, or technical fields can apply.

What are the key topics covered?

Topics include drilling operations, heat transfer, petroleum geology, fluid mechanics, and production engineering.

Will I receive a certification?

Yes, you will receive a recognized diploma upon successful course completion.

Graduates can work as Drilling Engineers, Production Technologists, Reservoir Engineers, or Field Operators in the oil & gas industry.
